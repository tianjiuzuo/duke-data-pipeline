Please read these terms and disclosure before continuing

The policy below applies to the following website:

www.dukedatapipeline.duke.edu

The ‘Duke University Domestic Violence Data Pipeline’ (“we,” “us,” or “our”), henceforth referred to as ‘Data Pipeline’, is committed to fairly and appropriately managing and safeguarding the information collected, used and maintained in support of its missions and to transparency regarding our data management practices. The ‘Data Pipeline’ respects the integrity of all its stakeholders, including as represented by personally identifying information about each stakeholder, and takes seriously its responsibility to protect individuals’ privacy in balance with meeting its legal, policy and administrative obligations.

The following describes how information is collected, used and maintained at the Data Pipeline. You may also refer to the privacy statement that covers all Duke.edu websites here.

Collection of Information

We may collect the following kinds of information when you use the Data Pipeline:

Information you provide directly to us. For certain activities, such as when you register, invited to participate, or contact us directly, we may collect some or all of the following types of information:

Contact information, such as your organization name and email address;

Username and password; and

Any other information you provide to us.

We may combine such information with information we already may have about you/your organization.

Information we collect automatically. We may collect certain information automatically when you use the Data Pipeline, such as your Internet protocol (IP) address, device and advertising identifiers, browser type, operating system, internet service provider, the date and time of your visit, information about the links you click, the pages you view, the general manner in which you navigate the Data Pipeline website.

We may use cookies, pixel tags, Local Shared Objects, and similar technologies to automatically collect this information. For more information on cookies, pixel tags, Local Shared Objects, and similar technologies, please see the “Cookies” section.

We may also collect technical data to address and fix technical problems to improve the Data Pipeline website, including the memory state of your device when a system or app crash occurs when using the website. Your device or browser may permit you to control the collection of this technical data. By using the Data Pipeline, you are consenting to the collection of this technical data.

Please note that we do not store the name of the organization point of contact. Only the organization name and the organization email address is stored to send email reminders.

Use of Information

We generally use the information we collect on the Data Pipeline to:

  • Provide and improve the Data Pipeline;
  • Contact you/your organization, including research results pertinent to you/your organization;
  • Analyze the domestic violence situation in North Carolina, potentially leading to the publication of research on trends and issues;
  • Fulfill your/your organization’s requests for information;
  • Prevent potentially prohibited or illegal activities;

Sharing of Information

We are committed to maintaining your/your organization’s trust, and we want you to understand when and with whom we may share the information we collect.

  • Authorized Duke professors and research assistants. We may share the data you share with us to Duke University professors and research assistants (who are generally students of Duke University). These individuals are bound by the confidentiality agreement, found here. This data will be used for research purposes, and strictly not for any commercial gain.
  • Legal purposes. We may disclose information to respond to subpoenas, court orders, legal process, law enforcement requests, legal claims or government inquiries, and to protect and defend the rights, interests, health, safety, and security of the Data Pipeline, our affiliates, users, or the public.
  • With your consent or at your direction. We may share information for any other purpose disclosed to you at the time we collect the information or pursuant to your consent or direction.

Security

We use reasonable measures to protect information from loss, theft, misuse and unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration and distribution. You should understand that no data storage or transmission of data over the Internet or any other public network can be guaranteed to be 100 percent secure.

We also request that any information shared by you/your organization be in an aggregate fashion, such that there is absolutely zero personally identifiable information entered regarding your clients. If such information is found to be entered into our database, we will initiate its immediate deletion.

Cookies

Cookies are small pieces of information stored by your device’s web browser. Pixel tags are extremely small images or small pieces of data embedded into images, also known as “web beacons” or “clear GIFs,” that can recognize cookies, the time and date a page is viewed, a description of the page where the pixel tag is placed, and similar information from your computer or device. Local Shared Objects are similar to cookies except that they can be larger and are downloaded to a computer or mobile device by the Adobe Flash media player.

We use cookies, Local Shared Objects, and similar technologies to enable the efficient operation of the Data Pipeline, to enhance the ease of use of the Data Pipeline, and to ensure that only authorized users may enter information on the website. By using the Data Pipeline, you consent to our use of cookies and similar technologies.

Updates to this Privacy Statement

The Data Pipeline reserves the right to modify all statements made about its privacy practices. We encourage you to return to this page frequently to check for updated information and effective dates. Your continued engagement with the Data Pipeline, after any changes to this Privacy Statement go into effect, in person or through our websites, constitutes your acceptance of these changes.
